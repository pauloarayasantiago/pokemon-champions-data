# Mega Froslass VGC Doubles Team — Regulation M-A

**Archetype:** Goodstuffs / Aurora Veil Offense with Snow-backed Mega Froslass as Mega and primary special win condition.

**Team at a glance:** Froslass-Mega / Incineroar / Sneasler / Rotom-Wash / Sinistcha / Kingambit
(Template inspired by tournament team PC124 — one of several successful Froslass builds this month.)

---

## 1. Mega Froslass — Mega / Snow Setter / Aurora Veil / Primary Win Con

- **Ability:** Cursed Body (base) → **Snow Warning** (on Mega Evolution)
- **Item:** Froslassite
- **Nature:** Timid (+Speed / −Attack)
- **Moves:** Blizzard / Shadow Ball / Aurora Veil / Protect

Stats post-Mega: 70 / 80 / 70 / **140** / 100 / **120** (BST 580). Mega-evolve turn 1 to summon Snow, which (a) makes Blizzard 100% accurate, (b) unlocks Aurora Veil, and (c) gives all Ice-types +50% Defense. 140 SpA + STAB Blizzard shreds neutral targets, Shadow Ball handles Ghosts/Psychics, Aurora Veil is team-wide damage halver for 5 turns. Outspeeds the entire unboosted meta short of Mega Aerodactyl / Mega Alakazam / Sneasler.

## 2. Incineroar — Lead / Intimidate / Fake Out Pivot

- **Ability:** Intimidate
- **Item:** Sitrus Berry
- **Nature:** Careful (+SpD / −SpA)
- **Moves:** Fake Out / Parting Shot / Flare Blitz / Throat Chop

Universal lead. Intimidate + Fake Out on turn 1 buys Froslass the tempo to Mega-evolve and set Aurora Veil turn 2. Parting Shot is Incineroar's only pivoting tool in Champions (U-turn and Knock Off are gone — see training-data flag below). Throat Chop blocks Perish Song, Hyper Voice, Snarl, Clanging Scales for the rest of the match.

## 3. Sneasler — Secondary Fake Out / Unburden Sweeper / Backup Win Con

- **Ability:** Unburden
- **Item:** White Herb
- **Nature:** Jolly (+Speed / −SpA)
- **Moves:** Fake Out / Close Combat / Dire Claw / Protect

Intimidate from Incineroar procs White Herb → White Herb is consumed → Unburden triggers → Sneasler sits at ~334 Speed and outruns everything. Close Combat is the nuke; Dire Claw is a 30% status flip (nerfed from S/V's 50% — see training-data flag). Fake Out adds a second turn-1 disruption tool.

## 4. Rotom-Wash — Bulky Pivot / Burn Support / Ground Immunity

- **Ability:** Levitate
- **Item:** Sitrus Berry
- **Nature:** Modest (+SpA / −Attack)
- **Moves:** Hydro Pump / Thunderbolt / Will-O-Wisp / Protect

Ground immunity covers Froslass, Sneasler, and Kingambit all at once. Will-O-Wisp neuters physical attackers Aurora Veil doesn't quite handle (Kingambit, Garchomp, Mega Dragonite). Hydro Pump hits Tyranitar and Excadrill — the Sand core that's Froslass's hardest matchup.

## 5. Kingambit — Priority / Fairy+Ghost Answer / Late-Game Cleaner

- **Ability:** Defiant
- **Item:** Black Glasses
- **Nature:** Adamant (+Attack / −SpA)
- **Moves:** Sucker Punch / Kowtow Cleave / Iron Head / Protect

Defiant turns enemy Intimidate into a +2 Attack boost — huge vs the 50%+ of teams leading Incineroar. Sucker Punch priority answers faster threats that punch through Veil. Iron Head punishes Fairies (Primarina, Floette-Eternal, Sylveon, Clefable) that wall Froslass's STABs. Kowtow Cleave is a 100%-accurate spam button that hits Ghost + Dark-weak targets for high damage.

## 6. Sinistcha — Redirection / Cleric / Rain Answer

- **Ability:** Hospitality
- **Item:** Sitrus Berry
- **Nature:** Calm (+SpD / −Attack)
- **Moves:** Matcha Gotcha / Rage Powder / Strength Sap / Protect

Rage Powder keeps Mega Froslass alive through spread damage and single-target threats. Matcha Gotcha is a spread Grass STAB that *heals your ally* (huge value with Froslass's 70 HP) and has a 20% burn chance. Strength Sap doubles as heal + Attack cut on the biggest physical threat. Hospitality passively gives your partner 25% HP on switch-in.

## Gameplan

**Primary plan:** Lead Incineroar + Mega Froslass. Turn 1: Intimidate chip + Fake Out the bigger threat + Mega-evolve Froslass to set Snow. Turn 2: Aurora Veil + Incineroar Parting Shot into Sinistcha or Sneasler. From there, Froslass 2HKOs most of the field under Veil-protected tempo while Sinistcha Rage Powders to preserve the Mega.

**Win conditions:** Froslass Blizzard spam under Veil → Sneasler Unburden clean-up → Kingambit Sucker Punch closeout if the first two drop weakened.

**Common leads:**
- *vs. Goodstuffs (Incineroar-led):* Incineroar + Froslass. Mega turn 1, Veil turn 2.
- *vs. Sun (Torkoal/Venusaur):* Sneasler + Froslass. Sneasler Fakes Venusaur, Froslass clicks Blizzard on Torkoal (Snow overrides their Sun if you Mega first!). Note: if they Mega Charizard Y, their Drought can clobber your Snow — lead Rotom-Wash + Sneasler and play for Hydro Pump + Close Combat on the weather setter.
- *vs. Rain (Pelipper/Basculegion):* Sinistcha + Froslass. Matcha Gotcha deletes Pelipper, Froslass Blizzards Basculegion (4x weak).
- *vs. Sand (Tyranitar/Excadrill):* Rotom-Wash + Incineroar. Hydro Pump + Fake Out the lead, bring Froslass or Sneasler once Tyranitar is off the field. This is the team's worst matchup — see below.
- *vs. Trick Room:* Incineroar + Kingambit (both slow enough to be OK in TR). Fake Out the setter (unless it's Hatterene — then Throat Chop). Don't bring Froslass turn 1.

## What This Team Struggles Against

- **Sand teams** — Tyranitar's Sand Stream *overrides* Snow on entry, shutting off Aurora Veil legality and the never-miss Blizzard. Excadrill's Sand Rush outspeeds Mega Froslass. Rotom-Wash + Kingambit Iron Head into Tyranitar is the plan, but it's a bad matchup.
- **Sun teams with Mega Charizard Y** — Drought sets Sun on Mega entry, overriding Snow. Venusaur's Chlorophyll (160 Speed) outruns Froslass. Lean hard on Rotom-Wash + Sneasler's Close Combat.
- **Hatterene Trick Room** — Magic Bounce blocks Taunt, and if she gets TR up, Mega Froslass's 120 Speed becomes a liability. Fake Out + Throat Chop pressure is your only answer.
- **Mega Gengar** — Shadow Tag traps your Ghost-resistant teammates. Froslass is Ghost-typed so it can switch; lean on that.
- **Steel walls that resist both STABs** — Aegislash, Archaludon, Kingambit mirror. Sneasler Close Combat is your best answer here.

## Where My Training Data Disagreed With Project Data (Flags)

| My default assumption (S/V era) | Champions actual (project data) |
|---|---|
| Froslass has no Mega Evolution | Mega Froslass exists — **70/80/70/140/100/120**, ability **Snow Warning** |
| Incineroar's core moves include Knock Off + U-turn | **Both removed in Champions.** Parting Shot is its only pivot move; Throat Chop replaces Knock Off |
| Dire Claw has a 50% status chance | **30% in Champions** (nerfed — still the highest-WR move in the meta) |
| Protect has 16 PP | **8 PP in Champions** — I kept Protect on 5 of 6 but don't plan to spam it |
| Fake Out usable any turn during active battles | **Unselectable after turn 1** — encoring into Fake Out just Struggles |
| 0 Speed IV Trick Room setups | **Impossible in Champions** — all Pokémon locked at 31 IVs |
| Life Orb / Choice Specs / Assault Vest are default picks | **All missing from Champions** — Mega Stones, berries, and type-boost items (Black Glasses, Mystic Water, etc.) dominate |
| Aurora Veil works in Hail | **Champions uses "Snow" mechanic** — same Veil rule (needs Snow), no Hail chip damage |
| Moonblast has 30% SpA-drop chance | **10% in Champions** (I avoided relying on it anyway) |

All sets, items, and moves above were validated against `pokemon_champions.csv`, `mega_evolutions.csv`, `moves.csv`, and `pikalytics_usage.csv` in this project.
