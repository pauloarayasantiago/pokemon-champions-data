# The Beginners Guide To Team Building For Pokémon Champions!

With Pokémon Champions dropping soon, there are going to be a lot of new players, possibly such as yourself, who want to learn competitive Pokémon. One of the biggest challenges when it comes to competitive Pokémon is team building. As fun as it might be to play with your favorites, that doesn't mean you can put them all on the same team and expect it to work. Unless they look like these. I genuinely have no words to describe how weird you are. I'm going to scoop back from you a little bit. These following categories of Pokémon can help build and shape your team to better fit your play style or patch the holes that your teams are missing. Keep in mind as well, these suggestions are based off the list of Pokémon that have been confirmed currently for Pokémon Champions, which you can check the link for in the description below. With that being said, let's get into it. Going first in any game puts you one step ahead of your opponent, and in Pokémon it's no different. The speed stat of all four Pokémon on the field help determine who's moving first, and that can be the difference of obtaining a solid lead or not over your opponent. Establishing speed control can come in a variety of ways. Tailwind, for example, is an amazing move that helps double your entire team's speed stat for four turns. This has the potential to make slower Pokémon faster than most standard speed tiers, and help faster Pokémon become the natural sweepers they were born to be. Amazing examples of Pokémon with Tailwind are Talonflame, Whimsicott, and Corviknight. Talonflame and Whimsicott are optimal picks because of their abilities, Gale Wings and Prankster, allowing them to use Tailwind first before anyone else. While Talonflame is riskier to go for since if it's not at full HP, it doesn't get priority to its flying type attacks anymore, it is faster than Whimsicott, allowing some mix-ups and counterplay depending on your build. Corviknight is also great because even though it's a slower Tailwind user, it has a lot of bulk and regeneration to keep it up while you set up your speed control. While being immune to stat drops, but we'll touch on that later. If you are scared about moves like taunt or want to be able to break focus ashes, Electro Web and Icy Wind are also great options as they're moves that can hit both opponents for a smaller amount of damage and lower their speed stats by one stage. If your opponent also has Tailwind, then this is a great tool to keep their speed stat down just enough while your monsters go burr. Swag. These moves don't need to be on specific Pokémon to be utilized well, but the faster or bulkier the user is, the better. Trick Room now is an interesting one as it swaps the natural turn order of all Pokémon on the field, essentially making it so Pokémon who have slower speed stats go first. I have a whole video about Trick Room, which you can check here or in the description below to understand it a bit more and why it's a great strategy overall. Solid Trick Room users that are confirmed for Pokémon Champions include Hatterene as it can't be taunted thanks to its ability Magic Bounce, which reflects status moves back to the user, and Farigiraf as it can't be fake out thanks to its Armor Tail ability, which prevents all priority attacks that would target your Pokémon from your opponent's side of the field. All in all, speed control is essential for getting your team off the ground, and I think these Pokémon are amazing picks to help boost you to victory. Redirection Pokémon are the literal punching bags of the game, but in a good way. Redirectors are able to send what could possibly be devastating attacks into your set up Pokémon or main sweeper onto itself instead. This tool comes in the form of moves like Rage Powder or Follow Me, both forcing single target attacks to the redirector Pokémon instead. These Pokémon are usually pretty bulky or have solid supportive tools to help you obtain your team's win condition. Say, for example, you have a Lucario out on the field beside a Sinistcha, and your opponent has a Krookodile. Even if both Krookodile and Lucario are max speed, Krookodile is always going to go first, and one high horsepower is all it's going to take to get rid of our aura farmer. Sinistcha, however, can rage powder beside Lucario, allowing Sinistcha to take the hit instead. And with its effective bulk and defensive typing into the ground type, it can allow Lucario to stay on the field and take out Krookodile instead. Redirector Pokémon right now can include the previously mentioned Sinistcha, who's great at sustaining itself and partner Pokémon thanks to the move Matcha Gotcha and its ability Hospitality. Other redirectors in the game to consider, however, include Maushold, which thanks to its Friend Guard ability, can help reduce damage to its partner Pokémon. Volcarona, who has a wide variety of tools to help your team like Tailwind, Will-O-Wisp, and Light Screen. Or Clefable, who wishes it was Clefairy, but isn't, proving that not everything gets better when you grow up. And to be completely honest, I can relate to Clefable. Prankster Pokémon can be devastating supports that can elevate your team. Prankster is an ability that gives a plus one to priority for all status moves used by the user, meaning that these Pokémon using status moves can move before other Pokémon. This includes a wide variety of moves and play styles such as the previously mentioned Tailwind for a fast and aggressive approach for your sweepers, or Thunder Wave to your opponent's speed stats and possibly stunning them for the turn as well. Prankster Pokémon are so good because of how they disrupt gameplay and try obtaining advantages for their side of the board. Popular Prankster Pokémon include the previously mentioned Whimsicott, but there's also Pokémon like Grimmsnarl, who can help set up screens to help with your team's defenses and hinder your opponents with scary faces, Thunder Waves, or parting shots. Sableye and Meowstic can also set up screens, but offer different support options such as Will-O-Wisp or Yawn, so take those into consideration as well if you're looking for a screen setter. Prankster Pokémon are strong tools that while are not completely necessary, can help add a variety of things to your team at a moment's notice, and should be considered if you feel like you need something and need it fast. Intimidate Pokémon are insanely threatening. Intimidate is an ability which when the Pokémon with this ability enters battle, it lowers both opposing Pokémon's physical attack stats by one stage. Lowering your opponent's physical attack stat can heavily hinder them as now it means they either have to spend a turn resetting their stats with a stat boosting move like Swords Dance or Bulk Up, Haze to reset all their stats on their board back to zero, or switch out that Pokémon entirely so they can use it at their full power another time. There's a reason why so many players have been resorting to the Clear Amulet item for certain physical attackers. They literally can't afford to waste time dealing with Intimidate. Even just the threat of it in team preview can make certain teams want to play differently, forcing their star attacker to be kept safely in the back until the intimidator is already on the field or is dealt with. Star Pokémon in this field surprisingly include both forms of Arcanine. As Kantonian Arcanine can be used more supportively with this ability thanks to moves like Snarl or Will-O-Wisp to keep your opponent constantly down in damage. While Hisuian Arcanine can use it aggressively, allowing it to come out, deal damage, and not worry about having to deal with incoming damage for the time being. Salamence is also great since not only can it start with Intimidate, but it can utilize a second ability when it mega evolves, allowing it to reduce incoming damage and dish out even harder hitting attacks afterwards. And that's it. No more intimidators. Yep. Totally no more. Sigh. Anti-stat deterrents are Pokémon that scare off stat dropping users like the previously mentioned intimidators, Icy Wind, or Electro Web users. Thanks to abilities like Defiant or Competitive, any Pokémon with these abilities that get their stats dropped while on the field, double their physical or special attack stat respectively. This can scare off opposing Intimidate Pokémon, allowing you to lead with a physical attacker that can run wild without having to worry about losing its attack [music] in fear of its partner Pokémon becoming even stronger than the intended target. Some Pokémon can ignore these stat drops altogether, however, such as Metagross thanks to its Clear Body ability. Defiant Pokémon like Kingambit or Competitive Pokémon like Milotic [music] or Empoleon are great examples of this as they already have solid attacking stats and can utilize them even more now being able to counter your opponent's stat drops. Corviknight, once again, is also an amazing deterrent thanks to its ability Mirror Armor, which reflects the stat drop back to the user, allowing it to weaken Intimidate Pokémon who switch in or punish Icy Wind or Electro Web Pokémon back to themselves while safely establishing bulk or speed control depending on the situation. All in all, they might be named deterrents, but they're pretty attractive picks when it comes to building your team. And there you have it.
