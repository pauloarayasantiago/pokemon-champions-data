## Archetype Meta Share
- Standard Goodstuffs: ~22% (most popular, flexible)
- Tailwind Offense: ~20% (Whimsicott-led fast offense)
- Sand Rush: ~19% (Tyranitar + Excadrill)
- Hard Trick Room: ~64% WR (highest performing archetype despite lower usage)
- Rain Rush: significant share (Pelipper cores are 20%+ usage)
- Sun Hyper Offense: rising (Torkoal + Venusaur is highest WR core)

## S-Tier Mega Evolutions
The strongest Megas that shape teambuilding decisions:
- Mega Dragonite: Multiscale (halves damage at full HP). Dragon Dance sweeper, extremely bulky first hit.
- Mega Clefable: Magic Bounce (reflects status moves back). Shuts down Taunt, Thunder Wave, Spore.
- Mega Meganium: Mega Sol (sets Sun on Mega entry). Sun archetype enabler, Grass/Fairy typing.
- Mega Feraligatr: Dragonize (Normal moves become Dragon at 1.2x). Extreme attack stat with Dragon-boosted coverage.
- Mega Gengar: Shadow Tag (prevents switching). Trapping ability for pick-off plays.
- Mega Charizard Y: Drought (sets Sun on Mega). Fire/Flying with boosted Fire STAB.

## Common Meta Threats Every Team Must Answer
1. Incineroar: Intimidate spam + Fake Out disruption. Answer: Defiant/Competitive users, White Herb (one-time stat drop negation).
2. Sneasler: Fast Dire Claw threatens status. Answer: Psychic types (4x weakness), Arcanine, Hatterene.
3. Garchomp: Earthquake + Dragon Claw spread. Answer: Ice coverage, Fairy types, Levitate/Flying.
4. Weather modes: Rain, Sand, Sun can overwhelm if you lack weather counter-play. Answer: Opposing weather, Cloud Nine, or type resistances.
5. Trick Room: If your team is speed-reliant, a hard TR team can reverse your advantage. Answer: Taunt, your own TR option, Imprison, or Pokemon that function in both speed modes.
6. Mega Gengar: Shadow Tag trapping. Answer: Ghost types can flee, Shed Shell (if available), U-turn/Volt Switch.
7. Kingambit: Supreme Overlord gets stronger as allies faint. Answer: Fighting moves (4x weakness), Intimidate before it sets up.

## Incineroar Deep Dive
The most used Pokemon deserves special analysis:
- 48-54% usage but only B-tier (sub-50% WR at times)
- Lost Knock Off and U-turn in Champions — major nerfs to its pivot utility
- Ability: Intimidate (97-100% of sets)
- Item: Sitrus Berry (58.8%)
- Moves: Fake Out (99%), Parting Shot (95%), Flare Blitz (85%), Throat Chop (41%) or Darkest Lariat (40%)
- Nature: Careful or Impish (max bulk)
- SP spread: Max HP, rest into Def/SpD
- Top teammates: Sinistcha (38%), Sneasler (36%), Garchomp (35%)
- Overcentralizing: Every team either runs Incineroar or has a clear plan to beat it
