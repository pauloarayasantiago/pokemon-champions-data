# Champions Rules - Regulation M-A

## Format Overview
Pokemon Champions VGC uses Double Battles exclusively. Regulation M-A is the launch format, active from April 8 to June 17, 2026.

## Team Composition Rules
- Bring 6 Pokemon, pick 4 for each battle (team preview shows all 6)
- All Pokemon auto-scaled to Level 50
- Species Clause: No duplicate National Dex numbers on a team
- Item Clause: Each item can only appear once per team (engine-enforced)
- 186 fully-evolved Pokemon available plus Pikachu (sole exception to fully-evolved rule)
- All Legendary, Mythical, Restricted, and Paradox Pokemon are banned
- Regional forms (Alolan, Galarian, Hisuian, Paldean) of eligible species are allowed

## Mega Evolution Rules
Mega Evolution is the ONLY battle gimmick in Champions. There is no Terastallization, Dynamax, or Z-Moves.
- One Mega Evolution per battle per team (team-wide restriction, not per-Pokemon)
- Mega Evolution persists when switching out — the Pokemon stays Mega
- If the Mega Pokemon faints and is revived (unlikely in VGC), it stays Mega
- Mega Evolution occurs at the start of the turn before moves execute
- Speed is recalculated after Mega Evolution on the same turn
- 59 Mega Evolutions are available; some popular ones are missing (Mega Salamence, Mega Metagross, Mega Mawile)

## Timer Rules for Doubles VGC
- Team Preview: 90 seconds
- Move selection per turn: 45 seconds
- Player total time ("Your Time"): 7 minutes
- Game time limit: 20 minutes
- Timeout resolution: The game is declared a DRAW if the timer expires, regardless of board state
- This makes stall strategies risky — you must actively pursue KOs

## Stat Points System
- IVs: All Pokemon have 31 IVs in every stat (locked, not changeable)
- There is no way to set 0 Speed IVs for Trick Room — all Pokemon have max Speed IVs
- Stat Points (SP): 66 total per Pokemon, maximum 32 per stat
- Nature: Called "Stat Alignment" — freely changeable, same 1.1x/0.9x mechanic
- PP: All moves auto-maxed using the formula maxPP = 4 * (basePP / 5 + 1)

## PP Changes
All PP is automatically maxed. Key values:
- 5 base PP → 8 max (e.g., Protect, Close Combat)
- 10 base PP → 12 max (e.g., Earthquake, Ice Beam)
- 15 base PP → 16 max
- 20 base PP → 20 max
- Protect specifically has 8 PP (reduced for balance)

- Type-boosting items (Charcoal, Mystic Water, Magnet, etc.)
- All Mega Stones
- Choice Scarf (likely available)

## Status Condition Nerfs
- Paralysis: Speed reduced by 50%, but only 12.5% (1/8) chance to be fully paralyzed (halved from 25%)
- Freeze: 25% thaw chance per turn, guaranteed thaw by turn 3 (was: 20% per turn, could last indefinitely)
- Sleep: Turn 2 has 33% wake chance, turn 3 is guaranteed wake (was: random 1-3 turns)
- Rest: User sleeps for 3 turns (increased from 2)
- Ice Beam: Cannot freeze in Champions

## Key Move Changes from Scarlet/Violet
- Fake Out: Completely unselectable after turn 1 (button greyed out). If Encored into Fake Out, the Pokemon uses Struggle
- Encore: Forces the target to use the Encored move at that move's priority bracket
- Salt Cure: Residual damage reduced to 1/16 HP per turn (was 1/8). Water/Steel: 1/8 (was 1/4)
- Leech Seed: Drain reduced to 1/16 HP per turn (was 1/8). Known bug: may still deal ~1/8
- Growth: Changed from Normal to Grass type
- Snap Trap: Changed from Grass to Steel type
- Dragon Claw, Shadow Claw, Dire Claw: Now classified as slicing moves (Sharpness interaction)
- Moonblast: SpAtk drop chance reduced from 30% to 10%
- Iron Head: Flinch chance reduced from 30% to 20%
- Dire Claw: Status chance reduced from 50% to 30%
- Screens in Doubles: Reflect/Light Screen reduce damage by 33% (was 50%)
- Unseen Fist: Nerfed to 25% damage through Protect (was full damage)
- Poltergeist: Now works on Mega Stones

## New Abilities
- Piercing Drill (Mega Excadrill): Contact moves deal 25% through Protect
- Dragonize (Mega Feraligatr): Normal-type moves become Dragon-type with 1.2x boost
- Mega Sol (Mega Meganium): Sets Sun on entry (personal Drought for Mega form)
- Spicy Spray (Mega Scovillain): Burns opponent on contact moves or when KO'd

## Known Bugs (as of launch)
1. Leech Seed calculates ~1/8 HP drain despite description stating 1/16
2. Lightning Rod fails to redirect Electric attacks if afflicted by Encore
3. Simultaneous Mega Evolution can cause priority/speed miscalculation
4. Quick Claw activates at erroneous intervals instead of stated 20% probability
