# Team Building Theory - Pokemon Champions VGC Doubles

## Type Coverage Matrix
A well-built team should be able to hit every type for at least neutral damage, ideally with super-effective coverage across the 4 Pokemon brought to battle. Key offensive types to cover: Steel (Fighting/Fire/Ground), Fairy (Poison/Steel), Dragon (Ice/Fairy/Dragon), Water (Grass/Electric), Ground (Water/Grass/Ice). Having both a strong physical and special attacker ensures you're not walled by one defensive stat.

## Role Compression
Role compression means using Pokemon that fill multiple roles simultaneously, reducing redundancy in a 4-slot bring. Examples in Champions:
- Incineroar: Intimidate (attack reduction) + Fake Out (turn 1 disruption) + Parting Shot (pivoting) + Fire/Dark STAB
- Sneasler: Unburden speed control + Fake Out (turn 1) + Poison/Fighting offensive coverage + Dire Claw disruption
- Hatterene: Trick Room setter + Magic Bounce (anti-Taunt/status) + offensive Fairy/Psychic STAB
- Garchomp: Ground/Dragon offensive coverage + Rough Skin chip + can run support moves (Rock Slide flinch)
The more roles each Pokemon fills, the more flexible your bring-4 options become.

## Speed Control
Speed control is the most important strategic axis in Doubles. Types of speed control in Champions:
- Tailwind: +2 priority, doubles team Speed for 4 turns. Set by Whimsicott (Prankster), Talonflame (Gale Wings turn 1)
- Trick Room: -7 priority, reverses speed for 5 turns. Set by Hatterene, Farigiraf, Oranguru
- Icy Wind: -1 Speed to both opponents, spread move. Reliable chip speed control
- Electroweb: -1 Speed to both opponents, Electric type spread
- Thunder Wave: Paralysis halves Speed but only 12.5% full para chance in Champions (nerfed)
- Sticky Web: -1 Speed on switch-in (rare in Doubles, strong against switches)
- Tailwind vs Trick Room is a fundamental matchup axis — every team must have a plan for both

## Win Conditions in Doubles
Every team needs at least one clear win condition — the path to knocking out the opponent's 4 Pokemon.
Primary win conditions in Champions:
- Weather sweeper: Excadrill in Sand, Venusaur in Sun, Basculegion in Rain
- Setup sweeper: Dragon Dance Mega Dragonite, Swords Dance Garchomp, Nasty Plot Hydreigon
- Trick Room Eruption: Torkoal under Trick Room deals massive spread damage
- Overwhelming offense: Tailwind + two fast attackers applying constant pressure
- Stall/chip: Incineroar + Sinistcha cycling (risky due to timer draw rule)
Secondary win conditions provide flexibility when the primary is countered.

## Bring-4 Theory and Team Preview
Team preview reveals all 6 Pokemon (species, form, item visible). You then pick 4 to bring.
Key principles:
- Build your team so that multiple subsets of 4 are viable (not dependent on bringing all 6)
- Identify your opponent's biggest threat and bring your answer to it
- Lead selection: Usually a speed control + attacker, or Fake Out + setup
- Back selection: Late-game closers or switchins for predicted threats
- Modes: Teams should have at least 2 modes (e.g., "Tailwind offense" and "slow pivot game")

## Doubles-Specific Tactics

### Protect
Protect is the most important move in Doubles. It blocks all attacks for one turn, allowing your partner to act freely. Champions changes: only 8 PP, and successive Protect fails at 1/3 rate. Use Protect to: scout the opponent's moves, stall out Tailwind/Trick Room turns, protect a vulnerable Pokemon while your partner sets up or KOs a threat.

### Fake Out
Fake Out flinches one target for a free turn. In Champions, Fake Out is completely unselectable after turn 1 — the button greys out. This is a HUGE change from S/V. Encore into Fake Out forces Struggle. Top Fake Out users: Incineroar, Sneasler (Unburden activation), Hitmontop (Intimidate).

### Redirection
Follow Me and Rage Powder redirect single-target attacks to the user. This protects your partner from targeted damage or status. Key redirectors in Champions: Sinistcha (Hospitality + redirect support), Togekiss (Follow Me + Serene Grace), Amoonguss is NOT in Champions (186 roster restriction).

### Spread Moves
Earthquake, Rock Slide, Heat Wave, and similar spread moves hit both opponents at 0.75x power. They're efficient for damage but risk hitting your own partner (Earthquake). Wide Guard blocks all spread moves for one turn. Rock Slide has a 30% flinch chance per target — in Champions with Iron Head flinch nerfed to 20%, Rock Slide flinch is more valuable.

### Intimidate
Intimidate reduces both opponents' Attack by 1 stage on switch-in. Incineroar is the primary user (48-54% usage). Counter-play: White Herb (restores one stat drop, consumed), Defiant/Competitive (punishes Intimidate with +2 Atk/SpA), Inner Focus (prevents flinch, not Intimidate).

## Item Economy in Champions
The restricted item pool fundamentally changes team building:
- No Choice Band/Specs: You cannot boost damage by 50% via item lock. Setup moves (Swords Dance, Nasty Plot, Dragon Dance) become more valuable.
- No Life Orb: No 30% damage boost with recoil trade-off. Damage output is generally lower than S/V.
- No Assault Vest: Special attackers are more threatening since no easy 50% SpD boost.
- No Rocky Helmet: Chip damage on contact is less available.
- Focus Sash is king: Guarantees a turn of action. Extremely valuable for fragile setup or speed control Pokemon.
- Sitrus Berry: The premier bulky item. Incineroar runs Sitrus 58.8% of the time.
- White Herb: Clears Intimidate drops. Key on physical attackers like Sneasler.

## Mega Evolution in Team Building
One Mega per battle is a team-wide restriction that shapes every teambuilding decision:
- Your Mega is often your primary win condition — choose it first, then build around it
- Consider what your Mega needs: speed control, redirection, Fake Out support?
- S-tier Megas: Mega Dragonite (Multiscale + Dragon Dance), Mega Clefable (Magic Bounce), Mega Charizard Y (Drought), Mega Gengar (Shadow Tag trapping), Mega Meganium (Mega Sol personal Sun), Mega Feraligatr (Dragonize)
- Your non-Mega Pokemon should complement what your Mega lacks
- In team preview, your Mega Stone reveals your Mega — opponents will plan around it
